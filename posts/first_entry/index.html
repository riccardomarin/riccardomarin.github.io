A little journal without any ambition

2021-12-02
2 min read
pills

I repent for not keeping a journal during my PhD. Annotating facts and thoughts is essential when dealing with complex processes. I do that for my research projects, but I do not for my academic experience. Curiously, I am also used to writing short tales but never related to my study path. I feel the urge to fill this gap.

Apart from the sentimental perspective (I enjoy reading notes I took years before), this regret has practical reasons. Sometimes I forget how I have solved certain situations or how an experience makes me feel. I can recall many factual things, but their taste is re-elaborated with today point of view. Probably, I am encountering situations I would skip if I can remember the consequences and the shortcuts discovered so far.

But more importantly, I met many people this year that makes me recall my own experience. I would love to have collected material to share with others as a concrete witness from my past self. My present re-elaboration of thoughts may filter important details, and I include several suggestions that probably make more sense to me than others.

Reporting your experience when you outlive problems (regardless of their magnitude) is important, but doing that while fighting to survive has a mighty emphatic capacity.

Keeping a little journal (without any ambition) is a good intention for the following year.
